# The 5 Largest Kuiper Belt Objects

## Brief Tour of the Outer Solar System

The Kuiper Belt is a broad ring of icy worlds beyond Neptune.  
By diameter, these are the five biggest known objects associated with the Kuiper Belt population.

---

## 1) Pluto

- **Estimated diameter:** ~2,377 km  
- **Flavor text:** The heavyweight icon of the belt, with nitrogen-ice plains and mountains of water ice.
- **Notable moon:** Charon (so large that Pluto-Charon is often treated as a binary-style system)

## 2) Eris

- **Estimated diameter:** ~2,326 km  
- **Flavor text:** A bright, distant rival to Pluto that helped trigger the modern dwarf-planet classification debate.
- **Notable moon:** Dysnomia

## 3) Haumea

- **Estimated diameter:** ~1,632 km (long axis is much larger due to its elongated shape)  
- **Flavor text:** A fast-spinning, stretched-out world with a ring and a collision-family history.
- **Notable moons:** Hi'iaka and Namaka

## 4) Makemake

- **Estimated diameter:** ~1,430 km  
- **Flavor text:** A reddish dwarf planet with methane ice and a very dark tiny moon.
- **Notable moon:** MK 2 (S/2015 (136472) 1)

## 5) Gonggong

- **Estimated diameter:** ~1,230 km  
- **Flavor text:** A large, distant, red-toned body whose slow spin hints at a complex past.
- **Notable moon:** Xiangliu
